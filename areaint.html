Areas of Academic Interest

Below are my areas of interest in literature and my philosophies regarding each.

Queer Literature

With "queer literature," I mean literature that uses LGBT people and concepts as its core. "Queer," itself is a difficult term because of the multiple meanings it has for different people. In this case, I am using it as an encompassing term of the identities under the "spectrum" (Though I detest spectrum). My philosophy regarding queer identity is not for the sake of giving something a label, but identifying and analyzing characteristics associated with the queer. My goal isn't to force a narrative but to present an alternative.

Young Adult Literature

In my graduate studies, I have found my interests leaning toward the not commonly studied, the "non-canonical" (That's a discussion for another day). My interest in young adult literature lies with subject areas being presented in a different manner than found in our traditional studies (Such as LGBT literature). I am interested in how underrepresented groups are viewed across the genre as well as the potential for challenging traditional representations found otherwise in historical works.

Video Game Studies

Much like with young adult literature, I am interested in the not as oft studied. In the realm of video games, I am interested in the ways games tell stories as well as how players perceive these stories. I am also interest in how the spaces within games are defined as well as the presence of pedagogical elements, such as scaffolding. As an example of the space of the game, I may look at Gone Home's atmosphere and use of lighting, sounds, and object interactivity to create a narrative that elicits forboding and mystery for the player. With games studies, I would look at what a game claims to do (Or what we claim it to do) and how it accomplishes that.
